Planning a trip to the bolivian highlands?

Uyuni Salt Flats

Salar de Uyuni is the largest salt flat on Earth, it is often called the “world’s largest mirror” because during rainy season (January to February), a thin layer of dead calm water transforms the falt into a big mirror with expectacular reflections where it is easy to lose the perspective of thing like moutains. When visiting Salar de Uyuni make sure to wear sunglasses, a hat and a jacket because the place is cold, as well, it is very convenient to wear rubber boots.

Rock Desert

Rock Desert is another amazing destination inside the bolivian desert, you should not forget your sun screen lotion here. Weather varies, it can be pretty warm during the day and get really cold at night, take your jacket with you and make sure you have at least two layers of clothes on. Wearing boots will be also a smart decision while shoes with thin soles may be uncomfortable.

Bolivian Desert

The weather inside the bolivian desert is a lot like the rock desert’s, the desert is home to some of the most beaautiful landscapes and mountains that you will ever see, here you can also find llamas, foxes, coyotes and rabbits, tourist are also amazed by the numerous and colorful lagoons, volcanoes, spring waters and geysers found in the area. Along with the outfits already described, you can also bring your short pants and t-shirts to swim in the spring waters. There is a small village inside the desert where tour operators accomodate tourists.
